Meet Nate

Born and raised in the Capital Region of New York, Nate Finke is a composer and musician seeking to contribute to the musical culture in the area through both writing and performance. Nate obtained his B.M. in Composition with a Concentration in Bassoon Performance from Ithaca College in 2022. Nate has also found opportunities to indulge his love for singing through jazz, barbershop quartet, theater, and more. His curiosity for the atypical and a passion for creative collaboration inspire and motivate Nate to create and share meaningful musical experiences.

Composer

The son of a musician and fascinated with music from a young age, Nate took great interest in music theory and arranging early on. In school, he engaged in musical extracurriculars and decided to study composition more seriously in college. During his studies at Ithaca, Nate expanded his compositional technique and voice while connecting with countless talented peers and mentors. Nate is well-acquainted with acoustic composition and orchestration, having myriad performances of chamber and large ensemble works. Electronic composition, synthesis, and audio recording and production proved to be equally compelling means for Nate to utilize when creating music. In his writing, Nate takes particular interest in the combination or juxtaposition of elements from varying styles of music in new settings.

Notable works and projects include a short piece titled "Breeze" (2022) for Arcomusical – a unique and relatively new ensemble of instruments inspired by the Brazilian berimbau – and over 10 minutes of original music written for a production of House of Desires at Ithaca College’s Dillingham Center. The music from this production will be performed at IC’s inaugural celebration of the newly-combined School of Music, Theatre, and Dance in October of 2022. Nate received the Louis Smadbeck Prize in 2022 for his song cycle, The Bells with text by Edgar Allan Poe. The full piece was performed at his senior recital in March of the same year.

Bassoonist

"Valsa da Outra Esquina", by Francisco Mignone

During his four years at Ithaca College, Nate enjoyed the opportunity to play bassoon in IC’s concert band, wind ensemble, and symphony orchestra. Ensemble repertoire ranged from classics by Beethoven, Ravel, and Rimsky-Korsakov to contemporary works by the likes of Karel Husa, Keiko Abe, Joan Tower, and many more. Nate enjoys and embraces every occasion to play in chamber and solo settings as well.

Vocalist

Nate Finke is a skilled tenor in various settings. For four years Nate sang with the Ithaca College Jazz Vocal Ensemble, performing at the 2019 NYSSMA Conference and attending a Jazz Education Network (JEN) session in 2021. The ensemble is also active in the Ithaca community, singing at churches and festivals in the area. Nate has been singing in barbershop quartets and choruses for over eight years, including with the Upstate Harmonizers chorus at the Barbershop Harmony Society International Convention in 2016. Nate’s most recent quartet, Catalyst, is preparing to qualify for the 2023 Youth Quartet Competition. Currently, Nate is a chorus member and chamber singer for First Presbyterian Church in Albany, New York.
